+My child breathing is noisy

The presence of a condition in which berating is noisy necessitating mouth breathing while awake and snoring while sleeping is suggestive of Adenoids. This is a lymphoid structure located at the back of the nose but can result in the above symptoms. There could also be associated bilateral ear discharge due to obstruction of the Eustachian tube orifices. It tends to regress at about 7 to 8 years of age but surgery might be required if there are the symptoms are worsening despite medical management.

+I'm experiencing bleeding from my nose

This is a common condition which affects both adults and children. In about 90% of cases, the cause cannot be determined. however while not caused by hypertensive heart disease, when both conditions occur it tends to be more severe. Pinching of the bridge of the nose and having a bowl to collect any discharge is helpful (mortified Trotter's position) and would resolve most cases but failure of which early presentation at a nearby hospital is of importance.

+My child experiences sudden-onset difficulty with breathing

This condition should raise the suspicion of a foreign- body in the airway especially in the pediatric age group.

+Myth 1: High blood pressure is accompanied with symptoms like difficulty in sleeping, nervousness and anxiety.

Truth: Hypertension does not have any symptoms. The only way to know you have hypertension is to have your blood pressure checked.

+Myth 2: Hypertension is associated with stress and excessive thinking

Truth: Hypertension is a multifactorial disease and not due to ‘thinking’ or stress. It is associated with significant disease of the arteries and veins of the heart.

+Myth 3: Hypertension is a disease of the affluent and the developed world

Truth: Hypertension is associated with both extremes of social strata. It is now becoming more prevalent among the low socio economic class where risk factor such as obesity predominates.

+Myth 4: hypertension can be treated by resting and adequate sleep

Truth: The treatment for hypertension includes lifestyle modification and the use of antihypertensive medications. Sleep has no impact on treating hypertension.

+Myth 5: one can stop using antihypertensive drugs once blood pressure is well controlled.

Truth: Antihypertensive medications are not stopped abruptly; otherwise the blood pressure will become elevated again. The drugs often have to be continued but can be adjusted overtime.

+What kinds of treatment do Physiotherapists administer to patients, if not drugs?

Core treatment procedures in Physiotherapy include; - Therapeutic Exercises, specifically designed to improve flexibility, strength, coordination, endurance, relieve pain, etc and ultimately to improve or preserve function. - Health promotion exercises - Use of Electro -physical agents like pain relieving and muscle stimulating electrical current, therapeutic ultrasound, heat, cold, laser therapy, etc. - Hands-on techniques like manual therapy, massage or soft tissue manipulation, joint mobilization and manipulation techniques, etc. - Drugs are administered only as topical creams
